Appeal letter — draft

Editable
Re: Appeal of Adverse Benefit Determination — Claim #48213 To Whom It May Concern, I am writing to formally appeal the denial of coverage for the above-referenced claim, denied on the grounds of "not medically necessary." This determination does not appear consistent with your own coverage policy. Specifically, Policy §4.2.1 states that this treatment is covered when a prior conservative treatment has failed, which applies directly to this case, as documented by my treating physician. Additionally, clinical guideline ref. NCCN-2.3 supports this treatment as an appropriate next step under these circumstances. I am requesting a full review of this decision and reversal of the denial. I have attached supporting documentation from my physician. Please respond within the timeframe required for a standard appeal under my plan. Sincerely, [Your name]

Privacy Policy

Last updated — draft, review before launch
In plain terms: we use your documents only to build your appeal. We don't sell your data. You can ask us to delete everything at any time.

What we collect

When you use Appealist, we temporarily process the documents you upload (denial letters, EOBs, and any supporting medical records you choose to include) in order to draft your appeal. We also collect the answers you give in the guided questions, the appeal letters we generate for you, your appeal deadlines, and basic account information such as your email address.

How we use it

We use your uploaded documents and answers solely to generate your appeal letter and track your appeal deadlines. We do not use your medical information to train AI models, and we do not share it with insurers, employers, or data brokers.

Where your data is processed

Your documents are sent securely to our AI provider solely to extract facts and draft your letter. This is a processing step, not a data-sharing relationship — your documents are not used by our AI provider to train their models.

How long we keep it

Your original uploaded documents (denial letters, EOBs, and any supporting files) are never stored after your appeal is generated. They are used once, in memory, to extract the facts needed for your letter, and are discarded immediately afterward — they are not saved in any database. You retain your own copy on your own device, since Appealist never needs to be a second copy of your paperwork.

We do keep the result of that process for as long as your account is active: the extracted denial reason, your appeal deadline, and the appeal letter itself, so you can revisit past appeals and track deadlines. You can request permanent deletion of your account and all of this information at any time from your account settings — this deletion is immediate and permanent, not a delay or a soft-delete.

Your rights

  • You can request a copy of everything we have on file for you.
  • You can permanently delete your account and all associated data at any time, directly from your account menu.
  • You can ask us not to use your information beyond generating your appeal.

Security

Data in transit is encrypted. Access to stored data is limited to the systems needed to generate and retrieve your appeals.

Questions about this policy can be sent to privacy@appealist.example.
